Page 1
======

Solar photovoltaic (PV) capacity worldwide surpassed 1.6 TW at the end of
2024, having tripled in five years. Utility-scale PV levelized cost of
electricity in sunny regions fell below USD 30 per MWh for new plants
commissioned in 2024.

Page 2
======

Onshore wind additions reached 117 GW in 2024, led by China and the United
States. Offshore wind grew more slowly than forecast due to supply-chain
constraints and cable-vessel shortages; total offshore capacity ended 2024
at roughly 82 GW.

Page 3
======

Geothermal enhanced systems (EGS) moved from pilot to early-commercial
scale in 2024, with a 3.5 MW plant in Nevada supplying grid power for the
first year of continuous operation. Levelized costs remain 3-4× solar PV
but the resource is dispatchable.

Page 4
======

Tidal-stream turbines contributed under 100 MW globally in 2024 and remain
pre-commercial. Biomass co-firing continued to decline in OECD countries as
coal plants retire. Grid-connected fusion generation remained zero as of
year-end 2024.
